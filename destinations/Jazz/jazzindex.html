Jazz is a genre of music that originated in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in African American communities in the Southern United States. It is characterized by a mixture of African rhythms and European harmonies, and its improvisational nature. Jazz music is typically performed by small ensembles, with an emphasis on improvisation and soloing. The rhythm section of a jazz ensemble usually includes piano, bass, and drums, and may also feature a guitar or other instruments. The lead instruments in a jazz ensemble are typically brass instruments.

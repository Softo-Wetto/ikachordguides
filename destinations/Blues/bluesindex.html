Pop music is a genre of popular music that originated in the mid-1950s. It is characterized by its catchy melodies, upbeat rhythms, and simple song structures, often incorporating elements of other genres such as rock, electronic, and hip hop. Pop music is often associated with mainstream commercial success and is widely popular across a broad audience.

Pop Sub-genres

Country Pop

Country pop is a music genre that combines elements of country music with pop music, often featuring catchy melodies, polished production, and a crossover appeal to mainstream pop audiences. It emerged in the 1960s and 1970s and has continued to evolve in popularity with artists such as Taylor Swift, Shania Twain, and Carrie Underwood.

J-Pop (Japanese)

J-pop, short for Japanese pop music, refers to popular music originating from Japan. It encompasses a wide range of musical styles and genres, including pop, rock, electronic, and more. J-pop often features catchy melodies, upbeat rhythms, and a mix of Japanese and English lyrics. It has gained popularity not only in Japan but also globally, with many J-pop artists achieving success outside of their home country.

Checkout Artists

K-Pop (Korean)

K-pop, or Korean pop, is a music genre that originated in South Korea and is characterized by its visually appealing music videos, synchronized dance routines, and upbeat electronic music. K-pop groups typically feature several members who are trained in singing, dancing, and acting, and the genre has gained global popularity in recent years, particularly with the rise of K-pop boy bands and girl groups.

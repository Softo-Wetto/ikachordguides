| Funk Section |

Disco, Electro-funk, Afro-funk, Boogie, G-Funk, Latin-Funk

Funk is a music genre that emerged in the late 1960s and early 1970s, primarily in African American communities in the United States. It is characterized by its syncopated rhythms, prominent basslines, and emphasis on groove and rhythm over melody. Funk is heavily influenced by soul, jazz, and rhythm and blues music, and is often associated with the use of brass instruments, particularly trumpets and saxophones. The lyrics of funk songs typically focus on social commentary and are often politically charged, addressing issues such as racism and poverty.

| Rhythm and Blues Section |

Soul, Neo-soul, Contemporary R&B, Alternative R&B, Motown, Afrobeat, Pluggnb

R&B (short for "rhythm and blues") is a genre of music that originated in African American communities in the United States in the 1940s. R&B music is characterized by a focus on rhythm and groove, with a strong emphasis on vocals and melodic hooks. The genre has evolved over time, incorporating influences from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz, gospel, soul, funk, and hip hop. Early R&B music was heavily influenced by blues and gospel music, and featured artists such as Ray Charles, Sam Cooke, and Etta James.

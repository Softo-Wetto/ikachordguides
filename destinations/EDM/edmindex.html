Techno, House, Trance, Dubstep, Drum and Bass, Future Bass, Hardstyle

EDM, or electronic dance music, is a genre of music that developed from the electronic music scene in the 1980s. It is characterized by its use of synthesizers, drum machines, and digital audio workstations to create a wide range of sounds and rhythms. EDM has become increasingly popular in recent years, with many sub-genres emerging within the genre. EDM is typically played at festivals and clubs, where it is accompanied by high-energy light shows and visuals. It is known for its ability to get people dancing and moving to the beat.
